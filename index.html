I am chooosing a dataset that contains information on student alcohol consumption. This data was taken from students that were found in math courses in Portugal. I would like to use this data to see what factors have the largest impact on an increase in alochol consumption and attempt to predict a students grade based on certain descriptions and drinking habits. I believe this is an important topic because alcohol consumption is very prevalent amont students in current times and is often very frowned upon as it is seen to have a negative impact on both health and grades. The attributes I will be looking at the most are "Walc" and "Dalc". Walc refers to weekend alcohol consumption while Dalc referes to workday alcohol consumption. Both columns hold values on a scale from 1-5 with 5 being very high and 1 being being very low consumption of alcohol. Since the students are taken from schools in portugal the grades are recorded on a scale from 0-20 with 0 being a 0 and 20 being a perfect grade.

Hypothesis: I predict that the more alcohol a student consumes, the worse their grades will be. I also believe the correlation will be quite strong with a 1 value increase in the Walc column resulting in at least a one point decrease in a students final grade. I also predict that studytime and absences will have a strong correlation with final grade.

Step 4: Future Work and Conclusion

4.1: Conclusion After looking at the violin plots, it is very apparent that using a students past failures is much more effective than using workday alcohol consumption in predicting a students final grade. Although it is more effective, this does not mean that it is very accurate. Both regression models have major flaws and for different reasons. Using a students failures to predict their final score is effective when predicting the grade of a student who will finish the school year with a poor final grade but not those with good final grades. This is most likely because if a student has failed either one or no classes in the past, you don't know if their grades have been anywhere in the range of a passing grade to a perfect grade. This can be seen in the violin plot as no final grade is predicted to be above approximately 12. As for alcohol consumption, there is undoubtedly a correlation between a higher alcohol consumption and lower final grades but not a strong enough one to accurately predict a students final grade. This could be the case due to the way the dataset recorded alcohol consumption along with survey bias. The legal drinking age in portugal being 18 years old mixed with the fact that the majority of the participants are under 18 years of age it would make sense that many of the students were dishonest when reporting the amount of alcohol they consume. Not only could this have skewed the regression models, the way that alcohol consumption was recorded also could have contributed. I believe that a scale from 1 to 5 with 5 being very high and 1 being very low is far too vague of a scale. A more accurate way of recording alcohol consumption would most likely result in more accurate final grade predictions.

I would also like to have more information on students past grades because I hypothesize that this is truly the best way to predict a students future grades.
